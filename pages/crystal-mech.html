Game Description

Crystal Mech is a game based on rogue-likes developed for the first UTS Game Studio. This project has also participated in the June 2020 showcase. From journeying through the deepest caverns. You play in traversing through the crystaline mines taking down enemies as you go. You defeat robots as you go you can earn weapons with greater power and earn increased abilities the deeper you traverse. However you'd want to keep yourself on your toes as your robotic enemies become much more stronger as you become strong.

Play in Itch.io See code in Bitbucket

Project Purpose

The purpose of this project is to develop a high-level project using the skills learnt from the subject. The Game Studio 1 subject taught a lot of important theoretical skills in games development and in software development. Throughout the duration of the entire games development course, the Agile/SCRUM methodology has been partly used to address the overall cycle and project management. This course explores through the different specific step by step areas in using SCRUM in our software/games development. Besides the overall premise of the game's result, the overall purpose was in experiencing the use of industry methods in project managemet and experiencing the benefits and challenges in following a process in software development.

Objective

The objective of this project is to create a fully functioning game that achieves the intended design defined during project planning. sprints or milestones.

Approach

When developing the game, our group has undertaken the extensive use of the SCUM methodology in managing our game development. Firstly we spent the early period ind eveloping plans and creating a design document and pitch that presents the overall idea and design of the game. From there undergoes a series of fortnightly sprints and multiple meetings in-between where relevant communications are held in discussing concerns, tasks/user stories and progress of assigned sprints. After a few periods of sprints deliverables are then finalised and a reflection following the Recount-React-Analyse pattern are completed

My Role

Throughout development, each individual has understanding multiple roles in development to both share the load and interface experiences of each member within that area. However for the most part, I've have heavily worked in both programming and artistic elements within development. This often involved developing multiple components for primarily the enemies and weapons. Additionally this has opened the oppurtunity in developing under the SOLID principles when both discussing and learning with other members of the group. Secondly, I've worked on some art that was created for both conveying designs of the game and for the cover art of the project. However my role in artistic elements extended over how the artistic design decided will be represented over the visual language and design of the game.

Personal Contributions

  • Development of improvements on present weapon systems including targetting systems and base weapon classes.
  • Post processing work on Unity's high definition render pipeline.
  • Weapon and experience particle systems.
  • Custom shadergraph shaders for HDRP unlit particles.
  • Modified kitbashed audio files for sound effects done through Audacity.
  • Custom crystal assets created through procedurally generated displacements in Blender.
  • Fine tuned lighting settings and added light probes.
  • Digital art created for both the cover art and the initial concept illustration.
