Animation Description

Growing Dark is a student animation created by Erin Heathcote, Jaiden Chicote, Cameron Bossalini and Brendan Micallef. It is a monster themed animation driven by the use of Quick Time Events to drive the progression of the story. The animation is created in Unity with the Universal Render Pipeline to drive the visual feel of the graphics. This animation was created for the 3D Animation subject in UTS exploring animation within a game engine and the many topics involving inverse kinematics, post processing and the animation timeline.

See code in Github

Approach

Considering as this animation the approach of developing animation required a modified approach to what was familiraily done in game development. Instead we combined some steps in animation to define the initial structure and ideation of the narrative before creation of the animation was created. Mjority of the creation of the animation utilised mostly asset implementation and animation timeline work, stitching animation sequences together and defining the differnet pathways in the QTE. However points of development did take place which were quickly developed and iteratied upon with simple prototyping methods.

My Role

My role in the creation of the animation involved visual effects and asset creation. Visual effects was an important role as it allowed for visual improvement in aesthetics and given more life to the elements within the scene. However there wewre numerous limitations in doing visual effects as the device used to develop these effects were inadequate in generating usable lighting without comprimising the framerate dramatically on the device. However working on asset creation allowed for custom designs of houses and scene elements that can be tailored to the scene aesthetics and environment. The reason this was necessary was due to the issue of assets taken from free and online sources to be extremely dense in verticies, making them unusable in scenes of multiple homes in a street. Additionally many of these assets did not meet the requirements for the visual aesthetic of the animation. Thus the need to create custom assets what crucial to the animation's progression.
