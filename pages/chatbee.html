App Description

Chatbee is an application that is designed for the elderly who have an inability to open up with people or to connect with new people. The application serves as a tool for indevidual elderly to converse with the younger generation or with friends on problems or experiences that enables for the users to reach out to people with more ease without the fear of meeting someone new.

The design of the application initially was modelled after common video chat, but was transformed to adapt story telling methods that can greatly serve the need for communication between recipients. In Aboriginal culture, yarning is very importnat as it is a form of storytelling. Yarning is an informal conversation between individuals either old or tyoung that are a means to talk about something, someone or provide and recieve information. This way of storytelling makes it esstential as it provides the incentive for the conversation to be initialised and enables senior users to reach out without fear or meeting someone new or beginning the conversation.

App Features

Chatbee included many features to provide for functionality that is inclusive to the needs of the user. With the nature of the subject involving user interfaces and interactioin design. Much of the features included are tailored to exhibit the necessity for responsive interaction and meaningful user experiences. Features include:

  • Universal navigation screen with large text for users with poor eyesight.
  • Friends list for quick accessibility for actions to communicate with known users.
  • Randomised chat with users using the application
  • Peer to Peer communciation for both voice and video call.
  • Accessible collage board with generated image alongside picture in picture video call
  • Collage scrapbook for records containing scrapbook images saved from calls between recipients.
