Game Description

Wormhole is a Unity-based simulation of theoretical wormholes developed for the UTS Games and Graphics project. A rather simple game on the first Look , the visual simulation is the core focal point of the gameplay and rather is developed towards a simulation rather than a game. On simulation, you'll see the two major components of the wormhole consisting in the internal projection and the outer gravitaitonal lensing. The internal projection utilses 2D manipulation of the camera image rendering the opposite end of the wormhole using azimuthal equidistant projection to remap the opposite view into a circular form. Secondly, the gravitational lensing seen on the wormhole's exterior utilises a modified version of the einstien ring formula for 2D manipulation that is applied as a way of deforming/displacing the color coordinates around the radius of the wormhole.

Project Purpose

The purpose of this project was as a research project conducted over the duration of the semester to study in detailor an attempt at original research that answers unique problems or concepts. Wormholes in Unity was chosen as a topic for the research project as it was the most interesting problem out of the three proposed ideas set before research had begun. Additionally it had felt approopriate as a learning experience in utilising both areas of HLSL or CG and Shadergraph as a way of creating these effects.

Objective

Successfully creating and demonstrating the visual likeness of a wormhole within a game environment in real time. Additionally to also develop a research report that reveals the choices discussed and the evidence used for the development of the wormhole.

Approach

The development of the wormhole has undertaken a prototyping strategy used in the Software Development Life Cycle but inclusive to the mulitple iterations of prototyping and refining of the core components of the wormhole. Additionally, the project had developed initial proposals that addressed the design, timelines, key goals and the deliverables expected for the project. This allowed for some structure to be followed when undergoing the development of the wormhole.
