Game Description

* Description from Itch Page

"Acquire all the main treasures indicated in each map to pass the level and pickup smaller treasures to improve your score. It won't be that easy though as enemies both on land and in the sea (and maybe at your ship) stand in your way."

The development of this game is done with a large team intending to for the game to be released. This game has also appeared in the 2020 November games showcase.

Play in Itch.io

Game Features

  • Multiple game levels navigated through a map-like interface in the main menu.
  • Capable 4 player multiplayer viewed all within a single map.
  • Dynamic ship damage system which also is revealed on ship after collision.
  • Custom water shaders for varying visual artistic features to compliment low poly setting.
  • Fully interactive vessel components allowing for modification or interaction of ship characteristics and functions.
  • Custom island setups and designs when traversing each level.
  • Custom audio soundtracks and audio implementation.

My Role

My role during the development was as another programmer and developer specialising in shaders. My involvement came in frutition after a month into the first half of the semester. At this point there was some help needed in team for developers to participate in development of the game and that the busy period of the second half of the semester meant that many of the developers would be busy on seperate projects. Additionally, development on shaders needed help as cetain areas of the game would require shaders to acentuate certain elements on gameplay. As such, it helped with the workload as other developers can then delegate more time to other crucial areas of development.

Personal Contributions

  • UI key prompts during events of player interacting with different stations on the ship. Directing players to the correct key prompts needed to activate the station.
  • Outline shaders experimented in HLSL and developed in ShaderGraph when being developed in the Universal Render Pipeline.
  • Development of camera shake functionality for ingame ship collisions.
  • Development of water shaders through the Shadergraph for sea/water related effects.
